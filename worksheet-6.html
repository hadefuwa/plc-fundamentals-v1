Worksheet 6 - Pump System Maintenance

1. Introduction

This worksheet covers maintenance procedures for pump system maintenance. Pumps are the heart of fluid systems, converting mechanical energy into fluid flow and pressure.

2. Over To You

Examine the pump system and understand variable speed control principles:

1

Identify Pump Wiring

Locate the pump in your training system and identify its 2-wire connection. Understand that at 24V across these wires, the pump runs at full speed.

2

Test High Speed Operation

Run the system and set the setpoint to 300 litres per hour. Note that at this setting, you would measure close to 24V across the pump wires with a multimeter.

3

Test Variable Speed Control

Reduce the setpoint to 40 litres per hour and observe how the voltage drops (likely around 12V). Understand that the pump needs at least 50% voltage (~12V) before it starts moving.

3. So What?

Understanding pump control methods is essential for maintaining efficient fluid systems:

Variable Speed Control

Pumps can be controlled proportionally by varying voltage (PWM) or frequency (VFD) instead of just on/off operation, enabling precise flow control.

Minimum Operating Thresholds

Most pumps require a minimum voltage/power percentage before they begin operating - typically around 50% for this system (12V out of 24V).

Flow and Pressure Regulation

Variable speed pumps allow precise control of flow rates and system pressure, improving efficiency and reducing wear compared to constant-speed operation.

Energy Efficiency

Running pumps at reduced speeds when full capacity isn't needed significantly reduces energy consumption and mechanical stress on system components.

Questions & Answers

Question 1: What is the function of a pump in an industrial system?

Correct Answer: A) To move fluids through a system by creating flow and pressure, supporting process control and system regulation.

Question 2: How can a pump be controlled to vary its speed?

Correct Answer: C) By adjusting the valve position.

Question 3: What might it indicate if a pump is running at full speed but still not meeting the setpoint?

Correct Answer: B) It means the pump is working perfectly.

Question 4: Why must pumps not be run dry, and what protects against this?

Correct Answer: A) Pumps use the fluid as coolant and lubricant; running dry can cause overheating or damage. A low-level sensor in the tank prevents this.

Question 5: What are some signs that maintenance engineers should look for to detect pump issues early?

Correct Answer: D) Higher temperature always indicates problems.

3. Interactive Pump System Maintenance

Understanding Pump Types

Centrifugal Pump

  • Uses rotating impeller to create flow
  • Flow varies with pressure
  • Better for high flow, lower pressure
  • Common in HVAC and water systems

Positive Displacement Pump

  • Fixed volume per rotation
  • Constant flow regardless of pressure
  • Better for high pressure, lower flow
  • Common in hydraulic systems

Common Pump Issues

System Leak

Watch how flow rate drops while power consumption remains high

  • Sudden drop in flow rate
  • Power consumption stays high
  • Pressure fluctuations
  • Possible fluid loss

Cavitation

Notice pressure fluctuations and increased vibration

  • Erratic pressure readings
  • Increased vibration levels
  • Noise and potential damage
  • Reduced pump efficiency

Overheating

Observe temperature rise and efficiency loss

  • Rising temperature trend
  • Decreasing efficiency
  • Potential seal damage
  • Risk of pump failure
