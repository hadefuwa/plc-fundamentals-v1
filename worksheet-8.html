Worksheet 8 - Float Switch System Maintenance

1. Introduction

This worksheet covers maintenance procedures for float switch system maintenance. Float switches are simple but essential safety devices that prevent tank overflows and pump dry-running.

2. Over To You

Examine the float switch system and understand level detection principles:

1

Locate Float Switches

Locate the two float switches in your training system. Identify the high-level switch (prevents overflow) and low-level switch (protects against dry running).

2

Observe Float Movement

Run the system and observe how the float switches move with the water level. Note their current positions - are they raised or lowered?

3. So What?

Float switches provide essential level monitoring and protection in fluid systems:

Low-Level Protection (NO)

The low-level switch is Normally Open (NO) and only closes when there's enough liquid. This allows the pump to run and prevents dry running damage.

High-Level Protection (NC)

The high-level switch is Normally Closed (NC) and breaks the circuit when the tank is full, stopping filling or triggering alarms to prevent overflow.

Safety System Integration

Float switches integrate with pump control systems to automatically stop operations when unsafe conditions are detected, protecting equipment and preventing spills.

Maintenance Considerations

Regular inspection ensures float switches move freely and contacts operate correctly. Debris or mechanical wear can cause false readings or system failures.

Questions & Answers

Question 1: What is the main purpose of a float switch in an industrial system?

Correct Answer: A) To detect liquid levels in a tank and trigger actions such as stopping a pump or sounding an alarm.

Question 2: What is the difference between a normally open (NO) and a normally closed (NC) float switch?

Correct Answer: B) A NO switch is always on, an NC switch is always off.

Question 3: Why is the low-level float switch normally open (NO)?

Correct Answer: C) So the pump runs faster.

Question 4: Why is the high-level float switch normally closed (NC)?

Correct Answer: D) So the tank uses less energy.

Question 5: What are some common float switch faults, and how can they be identified?

Correct Answer: B) High temperature, low pressure, or electrical noise.
